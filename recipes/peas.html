Black Eyed Peas

A southern staple and wallet saver.

When i was growing up, black eyed peas were something we always had on new years to bring luck for the comeing year. They are one of my favorite foods and now that i live on my own (with my husband) i make them all the time since they are cheap easy and dont require much prep work.

The process is very simple, and honestly you dont even have to soak the beans if useing a slow cooker. Below i will list the ingrediants, just throw everything in the crock and cook on high for 4 hours.

ingredients:

  • 1 pound dried black-eyed peas
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound smoked ham hocks
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon seasoned salt
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon crushed red pepper
  • 3 bay leaves

Steps:

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Soak black-eyed peas in a bowl of water for at least 2 hours, or overnight.
  3. Heat oil in a large, heavy saucepan over medium heat. Add onion and garlic; sauté until onion is translucent, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add ham hocks and water; bring to a boil. Cover, reduce the heat to a simmer, and cook for 1 hour.
  5. Drain peas; add to the pan along with seasoned salt, onion powder, cumin, black pepper, red pepper, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil. Cover, reduce the heat to a simmer, and cook for 1 hour.
  6. Uncover the pan and cook until peas are tender, about 30 more minutes. Remove bay leaves and ham hocks before serving.
